Patients need to understand their payment options and insurance types accepted to make informed decisions about their healthcare. A healthcare website's insurance and payment section should provide clear and concise information about co-pays, deductibles, billing, payment options, financial assistance programs, pricing transparency, and insurance pre-authorization. Patients should also have access to resources that explain insurance and payment options in detail and provide answers to frequently asked questions. This section can help patients avoid surprises in billing and ensure they receive the care they need without financial hardship.

Medical records and privacy are essential components of healthcare services. A healthcare website's medical records and privacy section should provide patients with information on accessing and requesting copies of their medical records, electronic health records (EHR), HIPAA privacy rules, patient privacy rights, and medical record release forms. The section should also describe how patients can amend their medical records and how long medical records are retained. Privacy and security measures should be clearly outlined, along with information on patient confidentiality agreements and data breach notification policies. Patients should also have access to resources explaining state and federal privacy laws, data privacy officer contact information, and patient rights to file complaints.
